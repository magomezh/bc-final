Global Super Store - A Case Study

Our team has been challenged with providing insights into the business of a retail organization. The dataset consists of business transactions over the last four years (2016 - 2019).

First Impressions

As true data scientists, we embarked on understanding and analyzing the data with a range of visualization tools at our disposal. Rendering their 2019 transaction data over a map we understand the retailer has a global business that seems to be doing well overall but challenged in certain markets.

Product Portfolio

We took the retail business' latest year financial data to understand their product portfolio, market penetration and sales performance. The sequences sunburst chart below presents our findings that is intuitive to read.

We learn that the business has products ranging from Phones, Copiers, Bookcases to Binders, Paper and Labels. These products are managed through three main categories - Technology, Furniture and Office Supplies. Notice that while the Asia Pacific market (orange segment in third ring) seems to be an important market with most Sales, the Western Europe region (light purple segment in outer ring) has a larger share of sales across all product categories.

About the Chart

A sunburst diagram displays a hierarchical structure. The origin of the organization is represented by the center of the circle, and each level of the organization by an aditional ring. The last level (leaves) are located at the extreme outer part of the circle. It is very similar to a treemap, except it uses a radial layout.

This particular rendering of the sequence sunburst is courtesy Kerry Rodden.
